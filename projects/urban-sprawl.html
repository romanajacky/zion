Urban Sprawl Analysis

Nairobi, Kenya | 2000-2020

Project Overview

This comprehensive study monitored urban expansion in the Nairobi Metropolitan Area over a 20-year period (2000-2020) using Landsat satellite imagery. The project aimed to provide insights for sustainable city planning and infrastructure development.

Key Findings

  • 156% increase in urban area between 2000-2020
  • 45% decrease in agricultural land within the study area
  • 200% growth in informal settlements
  • Urban expansion rate: 7.8% annually since 2010

Methodology

Data Used

Landsat 5, 7, and 8 imagery (2000, 2005, 2010, 2015, 2020)

Classification Method

Supervised classification using Random Forest algorithm with 92% overall accuracy

Change Detection

Post-classification comparison to identify land cover transitions

Impact & Recommendations

  • Identified priority areas for green space preservation
  • Recommended infrastructure expansion corridors
  • Highlighted flood-prone informal settlements requiring intervention
  • Provided baseline data for Nairobi's 2050 master plan
