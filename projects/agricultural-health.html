Agricultural Health Assessment

Rift Valley, Kenya | 2024 Growing Season

Project Overview

This project monitored crop health across 50,000 hectares in Kenya's Rift Valley using NDVI analysis from Sentinel-2 imagery, identifying stressed areas and predicting harvest yields.

Crop Health Status

  • ● Good: 35,000 hectares (70%) - Expected yield: 4.2 tons/hectare
  • ● Warning: 10,000 hectares (20%) - Expected yield: 2.8 tons/hectare
  • ● Critical: 5,000 hectares (10%) - Expected yield: 1.1 tons/hectare

Methodology

Vegetation Indices

NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) time-series analysis from February-June 2024

Stress Detection

Identified drought stress, nutrient deficiency, and pest infestation patterns

Yield Prediction

Machine learning model trained on 5 years of historical yield data

Recommendations

  • Critical zones: Immediate irrigation intervention recommended
  • Warning zones: Targeted fertilizer application
  • Healthy zones: Continue standard practices, monitor for changes

Project Impact

  • Prevented estimated $2.5M in crop losses through early intervention
  • Supported 12,000+ farmers with zone-specific recommendations
  • Reduced fertilizer waste by 15% through targeted application
